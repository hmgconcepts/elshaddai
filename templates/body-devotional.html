1

The Price You Couldn't Pay

Romans 5:8 · from the drama "PAMILERIN"

"But God commendeth his love toward us, in that, while we were yet sinners, Christ died for us." — Romans 5:8

In our drama PAMILERIN, a man faced a debt he could never settle — until another stepped in and paid it all. That is the gospel in one scene. Sin left every one of us owing a debt no money, morality or religion could clear. On the cross, Jesus wrote "PAID IN FULL" over your account (John 19:30, "It is finished").

Stop trying to earn what has already been purchased. Receive it. Rest in it. Live gratefully because of it.

🙏 Prayer: Lord Jesus, thank You for paying the price I could never pay. I receive Your finished work over my life today. Amen.

2

Mercy Found Me

Lamentations 3:22–23 · from the drama "MORENIKEJI"

"It is of the LORD's mercies that we are not consumed, because his compassions fail not. They are new every morning." — Lamentations 3:22–23

MORENIKEJI is a tale of mercy and intervention — of God stepping in exactly when destruction seemed certain. Look back at your own story: the accident that didn't happen, the door that opened at the last minute. That was not luck. That was mercy.

Mercy is not a one-time event; it is renewed every morning. Whatever yesterday looked like, today's supply of mercy is fresh.

🙏 Prayer: Father, thank You for the mercies that have kept me. Let Your mercy speak for me and my family today. Amen.

3

The Cheap Choice That Costs Everything

Genesis 25:34 · from the drama "TOKUNBO"

"Then Jacob gave Esau bread and pottage of lentiles… thus Esau despised his birthright." — Genesis 25:34

In TOKUNBO, a "cheap choice" ends up costing everything — just like Esau trading a destiny for a plate of food. The enemy rarely asks you to sell your soul in one transaction; he offers small, cheap compromises: a cut corner here, a little lie there.

Ask yourself today: what "small" compromise is quietly negotiating for my birthright? Shut the deal down before it closes.

🙏 Prayer: Lord, open my eyes to every cheap choice with an expensive price tag. Give me grace to choose destiny over appetite. Amen.

4

One Bite, One Mistake

1 Peter 5:8 · from the drama "THE POISONED FOOD"

"Be sober, be vigilant; because your adversary the devil, as a roaring lion, walketh about, seeking whom he may devour." — 1 Peter 5:8

Eden fell on one bite. THE POISONED FOOD reminds us that not everything attractive is safe, and not everyone smiling at your table is for you. Discernment is not paranoia — it is spiritual sobriety.

Before you "eat" — before you sign, agree, follow, or commit — pray. The Holy Spirit is willing to flag what your eyes cannot see (John 16:13).

🙏 Prayer: Holy Spirit, sharpen my discernment. Let me never swallow what was sent to destroy me — physically or spiritually. Amen.

5

This World No Balance

1 John 2:17 · from the drama "THIS WORLD NO BALANCE"

"And the world passeth away, and the lust thereof: but he that doeth the will of God abideth for ever." — 1 John 2:17

Our short film says it the way the street says it: this world no balance. Injustice, inequality, things that don't add up. The believer is not promised a balanced world — but an unshakeable Kingdom (Hebrews 12:28).

Don't anchor your joy to a world that wobbles. Anchor it to the will of God, which outlasts everything.

🙏 Prayer: Father, when the world doesn't add up, keep my heart anchored in You and Your unshakeable Kingdom. Amen.

6

The Debt Called "Gbese"

Matthew 6:12 · from the drama "GBESE"

"And forgive us our debts, as we forgive our debtors." — Matthew 6:12

GBESE — debt — is more than money. There are emotional debts: grudges we keep "owing" people, offences we refuse to release. Unforgiveness is an unfinished meal that poisons the one still eating it.

Today, cancel somebody's debt. Forgive — not because they deserve it, but because you were forgiven a greater "gbese" at Calvary.

🙏 Prayer: Lord, as You cancelled my debt, I release everyone who owes me an apology. Set my heart free. Amen.

7

The Mirror — Behind the Reflection

James 1:23–25 · from the upcoming drama "THE MIRROR"

"For if any be a hearer of the word, and not a doer, he is like unto a man beholding his natural face in a glass…" — James 1:23

Our upcoming film THE MIRROR — Behind the Reflection is a story of truth, self-examination and redemption. The Word of God is the only mirror that shows not your face, but your heart.

Look honestly into it today. Don't walk away and forget what you saw — adjust, repent, realign. The blessing is for the doer (James 1:25).

🙏 Prayer: Father, give me courage to face what Your Word reveals about me — and grace to be a doer, not a forgetful hearer. Amen.
